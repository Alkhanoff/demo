Bower has produced the majority of its equipment, excluding leased equipment. Production overheads were not included in the cost of the equipment under local GAAP. The overheads are 50% of direct costs.

The equipment was acquired in 1999 for €20 million at direct cost and depreciated using the straight-line method over 10 years under both local GAAP as well as IFRS. The fair value as of 1 January 2001 is €26 million.

This table shows the carrying amounts of equipment as of the end of each fiscal year under various accounting scenarios.

Amount in the opening IFRS statement of financial position at 1 January 2006 is €10.

The carrying amount under local GAAP of the equipment immediately after the business combination is deemed cost , in terms of the requirements of IFRS 1 relating to Business Combinations (contained in Appendix C), which is €16. The fact that this amount is not in compliance with IFRS (production overheads not included in the cost) does not alter the result.

As equipment requires a cost-based measurement, the deemed cost shall be the basis for amortisation under IFRS from the date of the business combination.

As the assessment of the useful life has not changed, the depreciation under IFRS would be equal to the depreciation under local GAAP as well as the carrying amount as at the date of transition.
