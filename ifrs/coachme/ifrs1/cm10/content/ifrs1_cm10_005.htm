What are the voluntary exemptions?
An entity may use one or more of the following exemptions:
- business combinations (IFRS3)
- Deemed cost (IAS 16, 40 & 38)
- employee benefits (IAS 19)
- cumulative translation differences (IAS 21)
- compound financial instruments (IAS 32)
- assets and liabilities of subsidiaries, associates and joint ventures
- designation of recognised financial instruments (IAS 39)
- share-based payment transactions (IFRS 2)
- insurance contracts (IFRS 4)
- decommissioning liabilities included in the cost of property, plant and equipment (IAS 16 & 37)
- leases (IAS 17)

- fair value measurement of financial assets or financial liabilities at initial recognition*
- investments in subsidiaries, associates and joint ventures
- financial assets or intangible assets accounted for in accordance with IFRIC 12 Service Concession Arrangements
- borrowing costs
- transfers of assets from customers
- oil and gas assets using the full cost method
- determining whether an arrangement contains a lease (IFRIC 14)

An entity shall not apply these exemptions by analogy to other items.

*IFRS 1 is amended to permit prospective application to transactions entered into 'on or after the date of transition to IFRSs', effective for annual periods beginning on or after 1 July 2011 with earlier application permitted.
