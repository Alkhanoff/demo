Recognition of resulting change to retained earnings
The first-time adopter...

...shall recognise any resulting change by adjusting retained earnings (or, if appropriate, another category of equity), unless the change results from the recognition of an intangible asset that was previously subsumed within goodwill.

...may have classified a past business combination as an acquisition and recognised as an intangible asset an item that does not qualify for recognition as an asset under IAS 38, Intangible Assets. It shall reclassify that item (and, if any, the related deferred tax and minority interests) as part of goodwill (unless it deducted goodwill directly from equity under previous GAAP).

...may have classified a past business combination as an acquisition and may have subsumed an intangible asset meeting the recognition criteria of IAS 38, Intangible Assets in goodwill. IFRS 1 requires the first time adopter to recognise and measure that intangible asset on the basis that IAS 38 would require in the separate statement of financial position of the acquiree. In that case based upon IFRS 1, the first time adopter shall decrease the carrying amount of goodwill accordingly (and, if applicable, adjust deferred tax and minority interest).

...shall recognise all other resulting changes in retained earnings.
