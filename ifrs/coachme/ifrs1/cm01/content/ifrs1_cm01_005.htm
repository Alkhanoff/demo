Glossary of terms
First IFRS financial statements Transition and reporting period Example

An entity’s first IFRS financial statements are the first annual financial statements in which the entity adopts IFRS 1, by an explicit and unreserved statement in those financial statements of compliance with the Standards. Financial statements under IFRS 1 are an entity’s first IFRS financial statements if, for example, the entity:

  • prepared financial statements under IFRS for internal use only
  • prepared a reporting package under IFRS for consolidation purposes

Date of transition to IFRS is the beginning of the earliest period for which an entity presents full comparative information under IFRS in its first IFRS financial statements.

End of the first IFRS reporting period is the end of the latest period covered by financial statements or by an interim financial report.

Entity A:

  • will produce its first IFRS financials for its annual period ending on 31 December 2010 in 2009, therefore 31 December 2010 will be the end of the first IFRS reporting period
  • will have one year of comparative figures, therefore 1 January 2009 will be the date of transition
  • has quarterly reporting requirements, therefore the first IFRS disclosures will be in the first interim report issued in 2010 that complies with IAS 34 - the 31 March 2010 quarterly report
