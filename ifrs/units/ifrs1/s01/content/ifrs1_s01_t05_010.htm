In general, IFRS 1 requires an entity to comply with each IFRS effective at the end of its first IFRS reporting period for its first IFRS financial statements, with some specific exemptions.

IFRS 1 also requires disclosures that explain how the transition from previous GAAP to IFRS affects the entity’s reported financial position, financial performance and cash flows.

The objective of IFRS 1 is to ensure that an entity’s first IFRS financial statements, and its interim financial reports for part of the period covered by those financial statements, contain high quality information that:

  • is transparent and comparable over all periods presented
  • provides a suitable starting point for accounting under International Financial Reporting Standards (IFRS)
  • can be generated at a cost that does not exceed the benefits to users
There are three key definitions that must be clear before continuing:
  • First IFRS financial statements
  • Date of transition to IFRS
  • First IFRS reporting period
