Garrett sums up: “IFRS 1 establishes some exemptions that a first-time adopter can use. These exemptions refer to:

  • business combinations (not to apply IFRS 3 retrospectively to past business combinations that occurred before the date of transition to IFRSs)
  • deemed cost
  • employee benefits
  • cumulative translation differences
  • compound financial instruments
  • assets and liabilities of subsidiaries, associates, and joint ventures
  • designation of previously recognised financial instruments
  • share-based payment transactions
  • insurance contracts
  • decommissioning liabilities included in the cost of property, plant and equipment
  • fair value of measurement of financial assets or financial liabilities at initial recognition
  • investments in subsidiaries, jointly controlled entities and associates
  • financial assets or intangible assets accounted for in accordance with IFRIC 12 Service Concession Arrangements
  • borrowing costs
  • transfers of assets from customers
  • leases (IAS 17 and IFRIC 4)
  • extinguishing financial liabilities with equity instruments
